About Chronica

A magazine dedicated to the stories, turning points, and forgotten corners of human history — written for the curious, not the credentialed.

Our Mission

Chronica exists because history is the greatest story ever told — and most of it goes untold. We believe that understanding the past is not an academic exercise but a human necessity. Every war, every empire, every plague, every revolution contains lessons that echo into the present.

We publish long-form articles, documentary features, and curated reading lists across every era of recorded history — from the earliest civilisations to the mid-twentieth century. Our editorial standard is simple: accuracy first, narrative always.

What We Cover

⚱
Ancient World
Egypt, Greece, Rome, Persia and the civilisations that built the foundations of the modern world.
⚔
Medieval
Crusades, plagues, cathedrals, and the slow forging of Europe from the ruins of Rome.
⛵
Early Modern
Renaissance, Reformation, exploration, and the age of absolute monarchs.
🏭
Modern Era
Industrialisation, nationalism, empire and the forces that shaped the nineteenth century.
✈
World Wars
The catastrophic conflicts of 1914–1945 that remade the political map of the entire world.

Our Writers

EC
Eleanor Cross
Editor-in-Chief · Ancient & Medieval
Eleanor holds a doctorate in classical history from the University of Edinburgh. She has written on Roman military strategy, Byzantine court culture, and the archaeology of the Black Death. She joined Chronica at its founding in 2026.
MV
Marcus Vane
Senior Editor · Early Modern & Military History
Marcus spent a decade as a researcher at the Imperial War Museum before turning to long-form history writing. His specialism is the military history of the early modern period, with a particular interest in the Thirty Years War and the campaigns of Marlborough.
SH
Sophie Hartley
Contributing Editor · Modern Era & Social History
Sophie is a social historian whose work focuses on how ordinary people experienced the transformations of the nineteenth and twentieth centuries. Her writing has appeared in History Today, the TLS, and the Guardian's history section.
JR
James Rook
Contributing Editor · World Wars
James is the author of three books on the First and Second World Wars, and has consulted on documentary productions for the BBC and Channel 4. He believes the wars of 1914–1945 remain the defining events of modernity and writes about them accordingly.
